Colorado Headwaters Ecological Spectroscopy Study (CHESS)

This is a landing page for accessing datasets and information regarding the Colorado Headwaters Ecological Spectroscopy Study (CHESS) - a remote sensing and field research campaign organized by Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory and the Lawrence Berkeley Lab Watershed Function SFA. CHESS brings together airborne imaging spectroscopy (hyperspectral) and LiDAR data with intensive field measurements of plant traits, soils, and geophysics to understand ecological responses to water stress in the Upper Colorado River system. The work is funded by The National Science Foundation, NASA and the Department of Energy.

Aerial Data Collection and Field Sampling Areas

Airbone Data will be collected in 3 domains encompassing the Upper East River, Almont, and Upper Taylor River. To keep with NEON's naming conventions, we will call these domains "CRBU", "ALMO", and "UPTA".

  • A map of the flight domains is available here: CHESS Flight Domains Map
  • Geospatial files with the updated flight boundaries are available here: CRBU geoJSON, CRBU KML, ALMO geoJSON, ALMO KML, UPTA geoJSON, UPTA KML.
  • Draft field sampling areas can be found here: CHESS General Field Sampling Areas geoJSON.

Flight Timing

The exact timing of flights is dependent on weather and the availability of the field team, but here is a general schedule:

ALMO - (Almont / Taylor Canyon vicinity, 9hr 23m flight time, starting ~6/11)

  • Fly in blocks roughly west to east, capturing low-elevation sagebrush communities (Block 1, lines 41 - 67) before peak greenness (late June), prioritizing flightlines 47 - 52 with field sampling areas.
  • Continue with Block 2 (lines 27 - 40), prioritizing flightlines 28 - 32 with field sampling areas.
  • Continue with Block 3 (lines 15 - 26), prioritizing lines 20 - 23 with field sampling areas.
  • Continue with Block 4 (lines 1 - 14) and cross-track lines to complete ALMO. May need to truncate survey of eastern part of domain (dropping Block 4) to start CRBU survey if this domain is phenologically ready.

CRBU - (East River / RMBL vicinity, 14h 17m flight time, starting ~6/22)

  • Fly in blocks roughly east to west, capturing low-elevation sagebrush communities (Block 1, lines 1 - 19) before peak greenness (early July), prioritizing flightlines 12-15 with field sampling areas
  • Continue with Block 2 (lines 20- 30).
  • Continue with Block 3 (lines 31 - 40). All lines are high priority because of dense field sampling.
  • Continue with Block 4 (lines 41 - 51).
  • Continue with Block 5 (lines 52 - 60). All lines are high priority because of dense field sampling.
  • Continue with Block 6 (lines 61 - 72) and cross-track line to complete CRBU. (could switch 5 and 6 to wait for phenology at Paradise Basin and North Pole)

UPTA - (Upper Taylor / P3, 8h 36m flight time, starting ~7/12)

  • Fly in blocks roughly east to west, capturing low-elevation lodgepole pine forest in Block 1 (lines 1-15), prioritizing lines 2-11 with field sampling areas.
  • Continue with Block 2 (lines 16-29), prioritizing lines 16-21 because of overlap with field sampling areas.
  • Continue with Block 3 (lines 30 - 40). All lines are high priority because of dense field sampling.
  • Continue with Block 4 (lines 41 - 69) and cross-track lines to complete UPTA.
